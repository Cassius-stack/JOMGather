Problem Statement
Bridging Intergenerational Interaction in Singapore

Background

In Singapore‚Äôs fast-paced society, interaction between youth and seniors has become increasingly limited. Many young people are focused on school or work, while seniors may feel disconnected from younger generations due to differences in communication styles, technology use, and social interests. Initiatives such as Youth Corps Singapore‚Äôs intergenerational programs, Lions Befrienders‚Äô digital buddy schemes, and school community service projects have shown positive impact ‚Äî but these efforts are often short-term and in-person, leaving gaps for continuous connection after events end. With the rise of digital communication, there is a unique opportunity to use web technology to create a sustained digital bridge between generations ‚Äî one that promotes understanding, storytelling, and mutual learning in a fun, meaningful way.

Problem Statement

Intergenerational connections in Singapore are weakening due to a lack of sustained, meaningful interaction between youth and seniors. This contributes to loneliness among seniors and reduced empathy and cultural understanding among younger generations.

How might we design a web-based platform that encourages ongoing, meaningful interactions between seniors and youth ‚Äî through shared experiences, digital storytelling, or collaborative activities ‚Äî in a way that is accessible, enjoyable, and sustainable?

üè° "Support Swaps" (Micro-Assistance Requests)
This feature expands on Zongrong's idea of Physical Assistance and Akil's Skills Marketplace by turning small, non-skill-based chores or errands into low-friction, high-impact bonding opportunities for the paired users.
Core Concept: A dedicated, hyper-localized system for the elder to request minor, non-expert physical assistance from their paired student, making the student a reliable support mechanism.
How it Works:
The Request: The elder can quickly post a pre-set "Micro-Assistance Request" to their student's private feed. These are limited to simple tasks that require only time and presence, not specialized skills:
"Help me organize these photos/books."
"Pick up a library book on your way over."
"Help me change a lightbulb or set up a new device."
"Walk my pet/water my plants for 15 minutes."
The Acceptance: The student receives a notification and can accept or decline. If accepted, the app suggests a meeting time (integrating with a calendar tool if available).
Reciprocity Log: These completed tasks are logged. For every five Support Swaps completed by the student, the elder is prompted to offer a "Legacy Gift"‚Äîa tangible item or service (e.g., a handwritten recipe, a small homemade craft, or an offer of a Boomerang Quick Call outside of their scheduled time) reinforcing the idea of a two-way exchange.
Safety & Scope: Clearly defined rules ensure tasks are safe, short (under 30 minutes), and do not involve money handling or heavy lifting.
Complementary Value:
It addresses the real, small needs of the elderly population that often go unmet.
It gives the student a powerful sense of purpose and utility, reinforcing the value of their time and physical presence.
It grounds the digital relationship in tangible, helpful real-world interaction, complementing the digital focus of the Activity Suite and Slice of Life.
